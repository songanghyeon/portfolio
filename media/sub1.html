About us

Australia's major cities include Canberra, the state capital, Sydney, the most populous state in New South Wales, Melbourne, Victoria's second-largest city, and Brisbane, Queensland's third-largest city. Located in the Oceania continent, Australia is the largest country by area. The continent is classified as the oldest, flat, and driest area, and the soil is not fertile. It has a variety of landscapes and climates, with deserts in the center, rainforests in the northeast, tropical savannas in the north, and mountain ranges in the southeast.

It is ethnically diverse, multicultural, and is a product of large-scale immigration, and according to data from the Australian Statistical Office, 29.1%, or about 7.5 million people, were born abroad as of 2021. Rich natural resources and well-developed international trade relations are very important in the economy and generate income from various fields such as services, mining exports, banking, manufacturing, agriculture, and international education.

Australian visa information

  • Do I need a visa to enter Australia?

    If you are not an Australian citizen, you must have a full Australian visa to enter the country. New Zealand passport holders can apply for a visa after entering the country. Other passport holders, regardless of age, must apply for a visa before departing from their home country. You can apply for several types of Australian visas, including tourist visas and working holiday visas, on the ETA app or on the Home Office website.

  • How can I apply for an Australian tourist visa?

    For visas, you can already create an account and complete the application process to apply online. To avoid a tight processing period, apply in advance in your spare time based on your travel departure date. You may need to provide additional support information. Once your tourist visa is approved, you will be connected to your passport in writing and digitally. For more information on the different visa types and Australian visa requirements, including how to apply for an Australian visa, please visit the Home Office website.

  • Can I extend my visitor visa?

    If you are already in Australia with an ETA visa (subclass 601), you can extend your stay by applying for another type of visa, such as Visitor Visa (subclass 600), which is a visitor visa. The eVisitor (subclass 651) visa is not extendable.

Working Holiday Visa

  • What is a working holiday visa?

    Australia's working holiday maker program allows visitors under the age of 30 (or 35 in certain cases) with passports from participating countries to travel and work in Australia. The working holiday visa is valid for one year or up to three years if certain conditions are met.
