Place

Go around places you can't experience anywhere

Sydney
Australia's largest city and capital of New South Wales, is renowned for iconic landmarks like the Sydney Opera House and Sydney Harbour Bridge. It offers stunning beaches, vibrant cultural scenes, and diverse dining options, making it a popular tourist destination worldwide.
Melburne
Melbourne, capital of Victoria, is famed for its arts, diverse culture, and culinary scene. Iconic spots like Federation Square and the Royal Botanic Gardens add to its charm. The city's mix of historic and modern architecture, along with its lively festivals, make it a must-visit destination.
Bondi
Bondi Beach, Sydney's iconic shoreline, is famous for its golden sands, turquoise waters, and surf culture. It's a magnet for beachgoers seeking sun, surf, and relaxation, with trendy cafes and vibrant street art nearby.
Whitsundays
The Whitsunday Islands, off Queensland's coast, boast pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. Ideal for sailing, snorkeling, and exploring the Great Barrier Reef, they're a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers.
Kangaroo Island
Kangaroo Island, off South Australia's coast, is famed for its rich wildlife and stunning landscapes. Home to kangaroos, koalas, and sea lions, it offers secluded beaches and scenic coastal cliffs, making it a haven for nature lovers.
Purnululu
Purnululu National Park in Western Australia is renowned for its distinctive sandstone domes, known as the Bungle Bungle Range. These striped formations create a surreal landscape, offering visitors rugged terrain to explore, stunning gorges to hike through, and ancient Aboriginal rock art to admire.
Karijini
Karijini National Park in Western Australia is famed for its stunning gorges, cascading waterfalls, and ancient rock formations. Visitors can explore narrow gorges, swim in natural pools, and hike rugged terrain, experiencing the park's rugged beauty and unique landscapes.
Margaret River
Known for its world-class wineries and stunning beaches. Wine enthusiasts can enjoy abundant tastings. The region's lush forests and limestone caves add to its natural beauty.

Festival

Only in Australia! Communicate and enjoy various cultures

  • New Year's Eve
    Sydney's famous New Year's Eve fireworks attract audiences from all over the world, with fantastic views of Harbor. It starts at Harbor Bridge and several ships around Sydney Opera House Harbor from 9 p.m. to midnight, followed by fireworks and festivals.
  • Hamilton Island Race Week
    About 250 yachts from around the world gather to take part in Australia's largest boat race. On the ground, the island's premier resort features an amazing lineup of fashion events, a luxurious champagne luncheon, and a festive program of cocktail parties enjoyed under the starry sky.
  • Parrtjima-A Festival in Light
    A festival takes place in the remote small town of Alice Springs and the whole city is lit up with colorful lights. It is Australia's only indigenous cultural event celebrating the world's oldest existing culture through fantastic light installations and interactive workshops, lectures, film, live music, and entertainment
  • Vivid Sydney
    As the host city of the world's largest festival of lights, music, and ideas, it offers spectacular sights. During the three-week winter festival, people from all over the world visit Sydney to enjoy light installations and see-through footage from some of the most iconic landmarks.

Nature

Australia's nature and animals are special and an experience
that you can encounter up close and not find anywhere else.

Australia is known for its diverse and breathtaking natural landscapes, offering unparalleled experiences for both nature lovers and adventurers. From pristine beaches and vast deserts to lush rainforests and unique wildlife, Australia's natural landscapes are both awe-inspiring and abundant. Featuring some of the most beautiful, unspoiled beaches in the world, Australia's coastline stretches for thousands of kilometers. With white sand and crystal clear water, the Whitsunday Islands are a paradise for sailing and snorkeling. While the iconic and vibrant Bondi Beach is popular with surfers and beachgoers, the remote beaches of Western Australia offer solitude and peaceful respite for those seeking natural beauty.

Australia's efforts to protect the environment are evident through numerous conservation activities and protected areas. Marine parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and heritage sites are sustainably managed with these natural treasures for future generations. From the wild wilderness of the Northern Territory to peaceful coastal areas, Australia's natural wonders offer endless opportunities for exploration and adventure. Whether diving in coral reefs, trekking through ancient forests, or basking in the sun on remote beaches, Australia's natural wonders promise an unforgettable and rich experience. KANGAROO
